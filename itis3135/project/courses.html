Course Reviews

Charlotte is actually considered to be one of the best cities in the country for disc golf! With 13 courses in Charlotte, and many more in the surrounding areas, there are plenty of options to keep your disc golf experience fresh.


Hornets Nest Park

Recently re-designed, Hornets Nest Park sits in Charlotte. Made popular through it’s tournament on the disc golf pro tour, Hornets Nest holes present a challenging, but fun round of disc golf. With the front nine early holes being mostly simple shots, it slowly bleeds into more technical and longer holes through the back nine. The water on hole 2 is the detriment to many players, but is still a very realistic shot to hit. Offering long and short tees, Hornets nest is a good park for any range of player skill. Difficulty: Moderate to Hard

Renaissance Park (Renske)

Renaissance park is home to 3 different course layouts. Renske, which is the shortest of the 3, is a pleasantly easy course that still presents interesting shots. Most of the holes are very short and the rough is relatively forgiving. The low challenge makes this course perfect for new players, and for more experienced players - a great way to have fun with this course is to limit your disc selection (think of it like mini-golf for disc golf!). Difficulty: Beginner

Renaissance Park (Silver)

Renaissance park’s “Silver” course is on the other side of the park from the Renske course. Completely different holes, the Silver course falls between Renske and Gold in terms of difficulty. Hole one on Silver presents a simple forehand shot or backhand turnover, but as the holes bleed into the woods the shots become much more technical with an unforgiving rough. Not only being able to push distance on some holes is very useful, but also controlling the speed of your disc is a good skill here as well. Renaissance’s Silver course is a great stepping stone away from more beginner and shorter courses. Difficulty: Beginner to Medium
