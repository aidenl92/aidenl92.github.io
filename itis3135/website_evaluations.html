Website Evaluations


Microsoft

Most of us are familiar with Microsoft as the company produces a wide variety of products: software, PCs, Xbox, and various consumer electronics. As expected, their website's design meets the CRAP principles.

  • Contrast: The text is very easy to read against its background. The color choice is also appropriate as there is not any clutter.
  • Repetition: The headings, text sizes, and layout is consistent across the page. The color scheme is also mostly consistent across all of the element backgrounds with the exception of things they really want to draw attention to (eg. Xbox game pass).
  • Alignment: The titles, headings, and elements are all well aligned, giving the page an organized feel.
  • Proximity: The proximity of text to the images make it obvious to the user of which text blocks are connected to which image.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc.

This company sells x-ray and radiology equipment. While this isn't a widely known company, their website is still very poorly designed and does not adhere to any of the CRAP principles.

  • Contrast: The contrast choice is terrible as the black text is difficult to read against the dark blue parts of the background.
  • Repetition: While navigating the site, one can almost immediately notice the many inconsistencies in the layouts of pages that should be very similar. This makes it difficult to quickly identify elements of the site.
  • Alignment: The images seem like they are aligned randomly, even making the text out of alignment due to the way the text is wrapped around the images. This gives the site a messy, unorganized appearance.
  • Proximity: Elements that are related have very poor proximity to each other on this site. There are even several instances where there are huge line breaks between text that is in the same paragraph element.
